1. Scope of Service

Caresy provides non-medical assistance and hospital companions. The service is strictly navigational, administrative, logistical, and social:

  • In-Hospital Navigation: Helping patients move between departments, diagnostic labs, scanning units, billing kiosks, and consultation rooms.
  • Queue Management: Standing in registration, consultation, billing, or pharmacy queues on behalf of the patient.
  • Administrative Support: Organizing medical files, filling out registration forms, and collecting physical bills or prescription sheets.
  • Note-Taking & Communication: Recording verbal instructions from doctors and sending milestone status updates to family contacts.
  • Companion Escort: Escorting patients to and from the hospital gates or their residences.

Plain English Summary

Our companions assist with standing in queues, finding hospital departments, filling administrative paperwork, writing down doctor instructions, and guiding patients safely.

2. Medical Disclaimer & Exclusions

CRITICAL EXCLUSIONS: NO MEDICAL OR CLINICAL CARE

Caresy is NOT a healthcare, nursing, paramedic, home care, ambulance, or telemedicine provider. Our companions are trained layperson facilitators, not certified medical professionals.

Accordingly, companions are strictly prohibited from performing the following activities:

  • Administering injections, setting up IV lines, dressing wounds, or monitoring clinical vitals (unless under direct supervision and order of hospital nursing staff).
  • Making clinical assessments, giving medical advice, prescribing medicines, or recommending changes to treatment.
  • Taking responsibility for clinical decisions or providing signatures on medical consent forms.

Plain English Summary

Caresy is not a medical service. Companions do not administer medical treatments, make clinical diagnoses, or sign medical consent forms.

3. Emergency SOP & Protocols

In the event that a patient's physical or mental condition deteriorates during the booking duration, the companion will activate the emergency SOP:

  • Alert the hospital duty nurse and medical staff immediately to move the patient to the emergency triage room.
  • Contact the primary family emergency phone number immediately.
  • Contact Caresy central operations for back-office coordination.
  • Exclusion: Caresy is not an emergency response service. The family and duty doctors retain sole clinical authority and liability during emergencies.

Plain English Summary

In emergencies, companions will alert hospital staff immediately and call you. They will assist the patient to the triage room but cannot make clinical decisions.

4. Bookings & Cancellation

All service requests are subject to dispatcher review, location limits, and companion availability.

  • Urgent bookings (Same-Day): Average callback time is 6 minutes, companion dispatch within 45 minutes of booking confirmation.
  • Planned bookings: Scheduled companion profile details are shared 12 hours prior to the appointment.
  • Cancellation Policy: Cancellations made within 4 hours of the scheduled start time will incur a convenience/late fee of ₹150. This fee is automatically charged to cover companion travel and availability costs.

Plain English Summary

Same-day dispatch starts within 45 minutes. Future bookings share profiles 12 hours early. Canceling less than 4 hours before the visit costs a ₹150 cancellation fee.

5. User Responsibilities

To ensure a smooth service delivery, users must fulfill the following commitments:

  • Provide accurate patient files, appointment timings, doctor name, and hospital credentials.
  • Disclose any mobility issues (e.g. wheelchair usage), memory challenges, or language requirements during booking.
  • Ensure that the family contact is reachable by phone throughout the entire duration of the service booking.
  • Maintain respectful and professional communication with companions. Harassment, physical or verbal abuse, or unsafe work environments will result in immediate termination of the service.

Plain English Summary

You agree to give correct details (doctor, hospital room, patient mobility), stay reachable by phone, and treat companions with safety and respect.
